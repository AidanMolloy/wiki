# General

1. The name of the society will be The UCC Networking, Gaming and Technology Society, hereafter referred to as “The Society” or “Netsoc”.
2. The scope of the society will be defined as:
    1. All forms of electronic games.
    2. Electronic hardware and software.
    3. All aspects of technology of interest to the members of the society.
    4. The management and running of Esports teams and events.
3. The aims of the society will be:
    1. To provide information on and awareness of all matters within the scope of the society.
    2. To provide and facilitate access and exploration of the subjects within the scope of the society to members of the society.
    3. To encourage and support the involvement of members of the society in different and lesser known elements of the subjects within the scope of the society.
    4. To allow members of the society to promote aspects of the subjects within the scope of the society to fellow members.
    5. To provide a range of services to students in an effort to:
        1. Encourage them to explore and learn about things normally outside their reach
        2. Improve and contribute to the overall student experience
        3. Ease the lives of The Society’s members
    6. Any services developed by the society will all be provided under an “Open-Source” license of some kind and should be open to auditing and inspection by the public as both a learning resource and to preserve fairness and transparency within The Society.
4. The duties of the society:
    1. The society will run on average a minimum of 10 events per term excluding general meetings.
    2. The society will run events from each major aspect of the scope of the society during the year.
    3. In the event that an Chairperson feels they are unfit to best represent a certain scope of the society, they may choose a sub-committee to represent the interests of Netsoc’s members within that scope. (Please refer to Section 5)
5. There shall be two classes of membership:
    1. Ordinary membership of the society, which shall be available to all University students and staff.
    2. Honorary life membership, which will be awarded to any Ordinary member of the society who is recognised as having made an exceptional contribution to the society. Honorary life membership will be decided by a popular vote of all eligible members at either an AGM or EGM. A list of honorary life members will be kept as an appendix to this constitution.

# The Committee

1. The Committee will ensure the smooth running of the society, the fulfilment of its duties and aims within its scope.
2. The Term of each Committee shall be from the 1st of June of the year of their election to the 31st of May of the following year.
3. The period from the AGM electing the Committee to the 1st of June will be a formal crossover period.
4. The Committee will be elected solely from ordinary members. It shall be composed of the following officials:
    1. Chairperson.
    2. Finance Officer.
    3. Secretary.
    4. Events Officer.
    5. Equipment Officer.
    6. Public relations Officer (PRO).
    7. Vice-Public Relations Officer (Vice-PRO).
    8. Two or more Ordinary Committee Members (OCM).
    9. First Year Representative.
    10. Head Systems’ Administrator.
    11. Two or three Ex-Officios.
    12. Gaming Team Members.
    13. System Administrators.
    14. Esports Co-Ordinator.
5. The title of Vice-Chairperson may be awarded to any member of Committee by the Chairperson.
6. Any Committee member may resign by communicating their intention to do so in writing or by email to the Secretary or Chairperson.
7. Any Committee member can be removed from Committee by vote of the Committee should they be failing in their duties. A simple majority is required; in the event of a tie the Chairperson has the casting vote. The removal of the Chairperson requires a two thirds majority. The Committee member in question will not have a vote but will be given the opportunity to speak in their defence.
8. If the Committee is not complete in the first term the Committee will ensure an EGM is held within four weeks to elect new members. If it is incomplete in the second term the Committee may appoint another Committee member or society member to the role. If the missing position is Chairperson or Finance Officer an election be held as soon as practicable.
9. The number of OCMs will be decided by the Chairperson at any General Meeting where an election for OCM position(s) is being held.
10. The number of Systems Administrators shall be decided by the Chairperson and the Head System Administrator.
11. The Esports Co-Ordinator is chosen by the Chairperson and two other committee members from a series of applicants. The limit to which applications are taken from is at the behest of those choosing the co-ordinator.

# Duties of Officials

1. All Committee members
    1. Have a duty to uphold the reputation of the society and to act in its best interests.
    2. Will train the next year’s Committee during crossover.
2. Chairperson
    1. The Chairperson shall be the official representative of the society at all public events and will be considered official society spokesperson and physical manifestation of the society at all times.
    2. The Chairperson will be responsible for the overall running of the society and considered ultimately responsible for the maintenance of the society, its membership and its good reputation in all the societies dealings during their term as Chairperson.
    3. The Chairperson will run all society events and chair all Committee meetings.
    4. The Chairperson may delegate duties to other Committee members where necessary to ensure the effective running of the society.
    5. The Chairperson will have a veto on all Committee decisions except the removal of Committee members.

3. Finance Officer
    1. The Finance Officer will be responsible for all the society’s’ finances, ensuring the society remains financially viable, creating and adhering to the society’s budget and all financial dealings of the society.
    2. The Finance Officer will ensure the swift procurement of funding from the societies guild for equipment as required where approved by the Committee.
    3. The Finance Officer will report on the state of the society’s finances at the AGM.
    4. The Finance Officer is responsible for seeking sponsorship arrangements within the local and international technology industry to further the financial viability of the society.

4. Secretary
    1. The Secretary shall maintain a record of all meetings of the society.
    2. The Secretary will be responsible for creation and maintenance of minutes of all Committee meetings.
    3. The Secretary is responsible for any necessary administrative functions of the society such as the maintenance of the mailing list and organising Committee meetings.
    4. The Secretary is responsible for the booking of rooms for events.
    5. The Secretary is responsible for managing sign up lists for any event which it is seen to be required.

5. Events Officer
    1. The Events Officer will be responsible for ensuring the smooth running of society events.
    2. The Events Officer will be responsible for venues and the wellbeing of all those in attendance during events.
    3. The Events Officer will be responsible for maintaining an up-to-date schedule of potential and upcoming events.

6. Equipment Officer
    1. The Equipment Officer will ensure the swift procurement of equipment for the society as approved by the Committee.
    2. The Equipment Officer will manage and maintain all society equipment and a record thereof to be available to members on request.

7. Public Relations Officer (PRO)
    1. The Public Relations Officer will be responsible for all the society’s publicity.
    2. The PRO, in conjunction with the Vice-PRO, will ensure that posters are designed and visible on campus two weeks before events as well as available to the rest of the Committee for further promotion.
    3. The PRO, in conjunction with the Head Systems’ Administrator and Vice-PRO will maintain the society’s online presence in so far as it relates to publicity of events to members.

8. Vice-Public Relations Officer (Vice-PRO)
    1. The Vice-Public Relations Officers will assist the Public Relations Officer in their duties.
    2. The Vice-PRO can, with the agreement of the PRO, be given responsibility over some aspects of the PRO’s duties. The PRO is to be considered Vice-PRO for these aspects.

9. Systems Administrators
    1. The Systems Administrators will be responsible for maintaining the society’s servers and all content contained on them.
    2. The Systems Administrators shall also be responsible for the society’s online presence, specifically but not exclusively the maintenance and continued improvement of our website and other facilities provided by the society.
    3. The Systems’ Administrators, in conjunction with the Equipment Officer, will maintain, develop and be responsible for the hardware and software of the society computers.
    4. Documentation
        1. All services and systems implemented by Netsoc are required to have an appropriate degree of documentation written by the relevant Systems Administrator so as to explain a system in such a way that a successor may replace them in the event that they are no longer available.
        2. All documentation must be stored in a central location that is easily accessed by any current Systems Administrator.
    5. A position can be set aside on the Sys Admin Team for a First Year Sys Admin to be chosen by an interview process after the EGM.

10. OCM
    1. The OCMs will be responsible for ensuring the smooth running of the society generally by aiding other Committee members as required.
    2. OCMs may be given specific tasks or events to manage exclusively by the Committee. This includes Committee roles not present in this document if required.

11. First Year Representative
    1. The First Year Representative will have all the duties of an OCM.
    2. The First Year Representative in addition will be responsible for the interests of first years on the Committee.
    3. The First Year Representative is also responsible for promoting the society among first years.
    4. The society will endeavour to expose the First Year Representative to as many aspects of the society as possible such as other Committee roles and the societies guild.

12. Ex-Officio
    1. The Ex-Officios will be responsible for providing assistance and guidance to the society from their experience on it.
    2. During Committee Meetings, the Ex-Officios have the right to attend but do not have an automatic right to vote. This will not affect their right to vote during General Meetings.

13. Gaming Team
    1. Help out setting up equipment on gaming events on Campus, and also help out where applicable on events outside campus e.g. LANs
    2. If any help is needed on the e-sports side of things, the esports officer will ask the gaming team to help out.
    3. Planning gaming events at the start of each semester.If there are gaming events with empty slots (or too many general gaming nights) the gaming team will meet up with the events officer to discuss any possible events.

14. Esports Co-ordinator
    1. The Esports Co-Ordinator would act as the Committee’s representative between the Netsoc Committee and The Netsoc Rebels Esports Players. The Co-Ordinator would liaise directly with tournament organizers and the Rebels Team Captains to ensure that the experience is as fluid and simple as possible for Players
    2. The Co-Ordinator would also be in charge of calculating the cost incurred by each team for the year ahead, and furnish the Finance Officer with the required information in appropriate time in order to ensure that they may be included in the budget/applications for funding.
    3. There would also be an added responsibility for the Co-Ordinator to inform the PRO’s of the latest results and Esports news that would be reported across social media
    4. What is important to note is that the Esports Officer will not be in charge of Esports, they will instead act as the Committee Representative in dealing with tournament organizers and Netsoc Rebels, and report back to committee.

# General meetings and Elections
1. The society will have one AGM towards the end of the academic year to elect the Committee for the following year.
2. The society will also have one EGM at the start of every year to elect the First Year Representative and any outstanding Committee positions.
3. The society may as necessary hold additional EGMs to conduct necessary society business.
4. Members of the society can mandate that the society hold an EGM for a stated purpose by presenting 20 members signatures to the Committee or the societies guild. This EGM must be held within four weeks of the submission of the signatures.
5. In all votes all ordinary members of the society who have attended a minimum of 3 events and all honorary life members of the society will be entitled to one vote.
6. A minimum of two weeks’ notice is required to hold either an AGM or EGM.
7. In addition the following positions have the following specific criteria.
    1. The Chairperson, Finance Officer and Ex-Officios are required to have been on the society’s Committee the previous academic year.
    2. Ex-Officios do not have the requirement to be current Ordinary Members of the society.

8. The Head Systems’ Administrator shall be decided by the Chairperson based upon their prior contributions to the society, CV, past experience and where necessary interview.
9. The Ex-Officio Positions may be decided by a majority vote by the Committee. The committee ought to ensure that all persons eligible for election to the position of ex-officio wish to be included in the vote.
10. Any election criteria will be considered void if no suitable or electable candidate is present under them or if they are deemed unreasonable by the Committee upon consultation with the societies guild representative present. The criteria will be voided one at a time ascending this section of the constitution.
11. The chairperson from the previous academic year will be deemed to be an ex-officio unless a majority committee vote is held otherwise.

# Sub-Committees
1. The Committee may create Sub-Ccommittees as needed for Projects or Special events.
2. All Sub-Committees will have a minimum of one society Committee member on them.
3. All Sub-Committee members must be members of the society.
4. No Sub Committee can last for more than one academic year without the Committee voting to retain it.
5. All Sub-Committees will be provided with a written mandate outlining their scope and objectives.
6. No Sub-Committee can take on duties or authority of the Committee.
7. Sub-Committee Membership will not count as Committee Membership except where permissible for awards.
8. A Sub-Committee can set aside a position for a first year representative to be elected at the EGM.

# Interpretation

1. Where ambiguity exists, the constitution’s final interpretation rests with the Chairperson.
